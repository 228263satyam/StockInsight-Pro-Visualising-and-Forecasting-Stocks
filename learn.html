What is Stock?

A stock represents a share in the ownership of a company and constitutes a claim on part of the company’s assets and earnings. The more stock you own, the greater your ownership stake in the company. Stocks are also known as “equities.”

Companies issue stock to raise money for a variety of reasons, including expanding or modernizing operations, financing new ventures, or paying off debt.

What is the Stock Market?

The stock market is where buyers and sellers meet to trade shares of publicly traded companies. It's essentially a marketplace where individuals and institutions buy and sell ownership in businesses.

In the stock market, shares of stock are bought and sold, either through exchanges or over-the-counter markets. The stock market is one of the most vital components of a free-market economy, as it provides companies with access to capital in exchange for giving investors a slice of ownership.

How Does the Stock Market Work?

The stock market works through a network of exchanges. Companies list shares of their stock on an exchange through a process called an initial public offering (IPO). Investors purchase those shares, which allows the company to raise money to grow its business.

Transactions happen when a buyer agrees to pay what the seller is asking, or when a seller accepts the buyer's bid. The stock market functions like an auction, with buyers and sellers negotiating prices until a deal is agreed upon.

Why Invest in Stocks?

Investing in stocks allows individuals to grow their wealth over time. Historically, stocks have provided higher returns compared to other forms of investment, such as bonds or savings accounts. While stocks can be volatile in the short term, they tend to increase in value over the long term, making them an essential component of any well-balanced portfolio.

Major Global Stock Markets

New York Stock Exchange (NYSE): Largest exchange in the world, listing blue-chip companies.
NASDAQ: Known for technology and growth stocks.
Tokyo Stock Exchange (TSE): Major exchange in Asia.
London Stock Exchange (LSE): Key European market.
Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) and National Stock Exchange (NSE): Major exchanges in India, where Sensex and Nifty indices track performance.

History of the Stock Market

The concept of stock markets originated in Europe, particularly with the Dutch East India Company in the early 17th century. The first official stock exchange, the London Stock Exchange, opened in 1801. The New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) followed in 1792 with the signing of the Buttonwood Agreement.

Types of Investors

Retail Investors: Individuals who buy and sell stocks for personal wealth-building.
Institutional Investors: Large organizations like pension funds, mutual funds, etc., that invest substantial sums of money.

How Stocks are Priced

Stock prices fluctuate based on supply and demand. Market capitalization, P/E ratio, earnings per share (EPS), and dividend yield are metrics that help investors evaluate stock prices.

Stock Market Indices

Popular indices include the Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA), S&P 500, NASDAQ Composite, NIFTY, and Sensex. These indices measure the performance of specific groups of stocks, providing an overall view of market trends.

How to Buy and Sell Stocks

Investors use brokerage accounts or online trading platforms to buy and sell stocks. Different order types include Market Order, Limit Order, and Stop Loss Order.

Risks of Investing in Stocks

Stocks can be volatile and subject to market, sectoral, and company-specific risks. It is important to assess liquidity risk and overall market conditions before investing.

Benefits of Investing in Stocks

Stocks offer potential for high returns, ownership in companies, and liquidity. Over the long term, they have historically provided better returns than bonds or savings accounts.
